# Kouign-Amman

Steps: 

Make the dough: In a large bowl, combine 300 grams bread flour (or all-purpose),
1.5 teaspoons instant yeast, and 0.8 teaspoons fine sea salt. Add 175
milliliters warm water (around 35°C) and mix until a shaggy dough forms. Knead
for 5–7 minutes until smooth and slightly tacky. Shape into a ball.

First rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth or
plastic wrap, and let rise at room temperature until doubled in size.

Prepare the butter block: Place the cold 225 grams unsalted butter, cold between
two sheets of parchment paper. Beat and flatten it with a rolling pin into a
roughly 15×15 cm square. Refrigerate until firm but still pliable.

Laminate the dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a 30×30 cm
square. Place the butter block in the centre at a 45° angle (like a diamond).
Fold the dough flaps over the butter to enclose it completely, pinching the
seams. Roll out to a 20×50 cm rectangle, then fold into thirds (like a letter).
Wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes .

Add sugar & second fold: Remove the dough, scatter half of the 150 grams
granulated sugar over it, then roll out again to a 20×50 cm rectangle. Fold into
thirds, scatter the remaining sugar, fold once more. You may hear crackling —
that's the sugar working its way in. Wrap and chill for another 20 minutes .

Shape: Preheat your oven to 200°C (fan 190°C / 400°F). Generously butter a 23–25
cm round cake tin or cast-iron skillet. Roll the dough into a circle roughly the
size of your tin. Transfer it in, tucking the edges under slightly. Sprinkle
with 0.5 teaspoons flaky sea salt (e.g. fleur de sel).

Final proof: Let the shaped dough rest uncovered for 20–25 minutes while the
oven heats. The dough will puff slightly. Bake: Bake for 30–33 minutes until
deep golden brown and the caramel is bubbling vigorously around the edges. The
top should look lacquered and amber.

Flip & cool briefly: Immediately and carefully flip the kouign-amann out onto a
wire rack or parchment-lined board (caramel side up). Do this quickly — if the
caramel sets in the tin, it will stick. Let cool for at least 15 minutes before
slicing. Serve warm.
